Biology Course Catalog

Evolutionary Biology - BIO 102 The first principles of evolutionary theory, classification, ecology, and behavior; a phylogenetic synopsis of the major groups of organisms from viruses to primates. 3.0 Biology Introduction to Biology - BIO 101

Upper Division Courses

Biochemistry

Structural Biochemistry - BCH 301 The structure and function of biomolecules. Includes protein conformation, dynamics, and function; enzymatic catalysis, enzyme kinetics, and allosteric regulation; lipids and membranes; sugars and polysaccharides; and nucleic acids. 3.0 Biology Chemistry Lab and Human Genetics in Modern Society
Biochemical Techniques - BCH 301A Introductory laboratory course in current principles and techniques applicable to research problems in biochemistry and molecular biology. 2.0 Biology Must be taken with Structural Biochemistry
Metabolic Biochemistry - BCH 302 Energy-producing pathways–glycolysis, the TCA cycle, oxidative phosphorylation, photosynthesis, and fatty acid oxidation; and biosynthetic pathways–gluconeogenesis, glycogen synthesis, and fatty acid biosynthesis. 3.0 Biology Chemistry Lab and Human Genitics in Modern Society
Nutrition - BCH 303 Elaborates the relationship between diet and human metabolism, physiology, health, and disease. 3.0 Biology Structural Biochemistry - 301
Chemistry of Biological Interactions - BCH 304 Nearly all interactions between organisms, including host-pathogen interactions and mate attraction, have a chemical basis. Plants and microorganisms are the dominant life forms on earth and remain a major source of pharmaceutical leads. Students in this course will utilize biochemical methods to extract, fractionate, and analyze plant and microbial compounds of medicinal and ecological significance including antibiotics, growth regulators, toxins, and signaling molecules. 3.0 Biology Nutrition - BCH 304
Advanced Topics in Modern Biology: Biochemistry - BCH 305 Students are expected to actively participate in course discussions, read, and analyze primary literature. 3.0 Biology Nutrition - BCH 304

Biogenetics

Molecular Biology - BGE 401 Molecular basis of biological processes, emphasizing gene action in context of entire genome. Chromosomes and DNA metabolism: chromatin, DNA replication, repair, mutation, recombination, transposition. 3.0 Biology Human Neurology - BIO 104
Molecular Biology Laboratory - BGE 401A Techniques in microbial physiology, microbial genomics, microbial evolution, and microbial ecology will be used to explore the role of microbes in industry, health, and the environment. 3.0 Biology Taken with Molecular Biology
Molecular Basis of Human Disease - BGE 402 An examination of the molecular basis of human diseases. 3.0 Biology Human Neurology - BIO 104
Virology - BGE 403 An introduction to eukaryotic virology, with emphasis on animal virus systems. 3.0 Biology Molecular Biology - BGE 401
Pharmacology - BGE 404 Basics of pharmacology such as drug absorption, distribution, metabolism, and elimination. 3.0 Biology Molecular Biology - BGE 401
Microbial Genetics - BGE 405 Course will consider the organization and function of prokaryotic genomes including content, DNA supercoiling, histone-like proteins, chromosomal dynamics (short-term and long-term), extrachromosomal elements, bacterial sex, transduction, transformation, mobile elements (transposon), epigenetic change, adaptive and directed mutation 3.0 Biology Molecular Biology - BGE 401
Medical Microbiology - BGE 405 Encompasses the increasingly important areas of viral, bacterial, and parasitic diseases and understanding the complex interaction between humans and infectious agents. 3.0 Biology Molecular Basis of Human Disease - BGE 402
Quantitative Principles in Biology - BGE 406 Course considers problems in biology that were solved using quantitative biology approaches. Problems will range from the molecular to the population level. 3.0 Biology Microbiology
Bioinformatics Laboratory - BGE 406A Bioinformatics is the analysis of big data in the biosciences. This course provides a hands-on introduction to the computer-based analysis of biomolecular and genomic data. 3.0 Biology Taken with Quantitative Principles in Biology
Structural Biology of Viruses - BGE 407 An introduction to virus structures, how they are determined, and how they facilitate the various stages of the viral life cycle from host recognition and entry to replication, assembly, release, and transmission to uninfected host cells. 3.0 Biology Quantitative Principles in Biology
Computational Molecular Biology - BGE 408 This advanced course covers the application of machine learning and modeling techniques to biological systems. Topics include gene structure, recognition of DNA and protein sequence patterns, classification, and protein structure prediction. 3.0 Biology Taken with Molecular Sequence Analysis
